The Legacy

A Currency of the Austrian Empire

Originally struck in Austria from 1740 to 1780, the Thaler was the primary currency of the Austrian Empire. However, its influence extended far beyond the borders of Europe.

It became critically important for trade with the Levant (parts of Turkey, Lebanon, Syria) and eventually became the most popular silver coin in the Arabian world and parts of Africa.

"After the death of Empress Maria Theresa in 1780, Joseph II permitted the Austrian mint to continue striking the coin with the 1780 dies in order to meet demand from the Middle East."

Since then, the Maria Theresa Thaler has been "restruck" for trade purposes at Vienna with the 1780 date frozen in time. It is one of the few coins in history to be produced continuously for nearly 300 years with virtually unchanged designs.

Understanding Variants

While they all say 1780, minute differences tell the story of where and when they were minted. There are over 150 known variants.

The Saltire (X)

Added in 1750, the "X" (Saltire) next to the date indicates the debased standard of the thaler. It became a permanent fixture on the 1780 restrikes.

Eagle Feathers

To distinguish mints (Rome, London, Vienna), numismatists look at the tail feathers of the eagle. The arrangement (1-2-1 vs 1-3-1) is a key identifier.
